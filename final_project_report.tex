\begin{abstract}
This report presents a complete simulation and control study for a three degree-of-freedom planar revolute manipulator assigned in ESE 446. The work spans five tasks. First, a full nonlinear dynamics model is derived and simulated in state-space form under multiple gravity and torque conditions. Second, a partitioned closed-loop controller is implemented to separate model-based cancellation from servo regulation. Third, spline-based joint trajectories are generated and tracked over an eight second mission profile. Fourth, external Cartesian force and moment impulses are mapped into equivalent joint torques through the Jacobian transpose relation, then injected into the plant to evaluate disturbance rejection. Fifth, a Cartesian-space controller is constructed and tested with independent commands in $x$, $y$, and orientation $\alpha$. Results include time-domain state histories, end-effector pose histories, error traces, torque profiles, and path plots. The final model set satisfies the assignment requirements and demonstrates consistent links between lecture topics, physical interpretation, and simulation evidence.
\end{abstract}

\section{Kinematic Model}
\subsection{Forward Kinematics}
The end-effector pose in planar coordinates is defined by
\begin{align}
x &= L_1\cos q_1 + L_2\cos(q_1+q_2) + L_3\cos(q_1+q_2+q_3),\\
y &= L_1\sin q_1 + L_2\sin(q_1+q_2) + L_3\sin(q_1+q_2+q_3),\\
\alpha &= q_1 + q_2 + q_3.
\end{align}
These expressions are used in every task for pose plotting, path verification, and Cartesian control error computation.

\subsection{Differential Kinematics and Jacobian}
The planar Jacobian maps joint rates into Cartesian velocity,
\begin{equation}
\begin{bmatrix}\dot x\\\dot y\\\dot\alpha\end{bmatrix} = J(q)\,\dot q,
\end{equation}
with
\begin{equation}
J(q)=
\begin{bmatrix}
- L_1\sin q_1 - L_2\sin(q_{12}) - L_3\sin(q_{123}) & - L_2\sin(q_{12}) - L_3\sin(q_{123}) & -L_3\sin(q_{123})\\
\;L_1\cos q_1 + L_2\cos(q_{12}) + L_3\cos(q_{123}) & \;L_2\cos(q_{12}) + L_3\cos(q_{123}) & \;L_3\cos(q_{123})\\
1 & 1 & 1
\end{bmatrix},
\end{equation}
where $q_{12}=q_1+q_2$ and $q_{123}=q_1+q_2+q_3$.

The force-domain relation used in Task 4 is
\begin{equation}
\tau_{ext}=J(q)^T F_c,
\end{equation}
with $F_c=[F_x,F_y,M_\alpha]^T$.

\section{Dynamic Model and State-Space Form}
The manipulator dynamics are expressed in standard joint-space form,
\begin{equation}
M(q)\,\ddot q + C(q,\dot q)\,\dot q + G(q) = \tau + \tau_{ext} + \tau_f.
\label{eq:eom}
\end{equation}

The inertia matrix $M(q)$ is assembled from translational and rotational kinetic energy terms using center-of-mass Jacobians for each link. The Coriolis and centrifugal matrix $C(q,\dot q)$ is computed numerically from Christoffel symbols,
\begin{equation}
\Gamma_{ijk}=\frac{1}{2}\left(\frac{\partial M_{ij}}{\partial q_k}+\frac{\partial M_{ik}}{\partial q_j}-\frac{\partial M_{jk}}{\partial q_i}\right),
\quad
C_{ij}=\sum_k \Gamma_{ijk}\dot q_k.
\end{equation}
The gravity vector is obtained from potential energy gradients. A viscous joint friction model $\tau_f=-B\dot q$ is included with diagonal damping coefficients selected for numerical damping and stable long-run simulation.

Solving \eqref{eq:eom} for acceleration gives the simulation state equation:
\begin{equation}
\dot x =
\begin{bmatrix}
\dot q\\
M(q)^{-1}\left(\tau+\tau_{ext}-C(q,\dot q)\dot q-G(q)+\tau_f\right)
\end{bmatrix},
\quad
x=\begin{bmatrix}q\\\dot q\end{bmatrix}.
\end{equation}
ODE45 integration is used for all runs.

At the required initial condition, the baseline dynamic terms are:
\begin{equation}
M(q_0)=10^3
\begin{bmatrix}
1.1126 & 0.4091 & 0.0618\\
0.4091 & 0.1860 & 0.0361\\
0.0618 & 0.0361 & 0.0101
\end{bmatrix},
\end{equation}
\begin{equation}
G(q_0)=10^3
\begin{bmatrix}
1.8476\\
0.4951\\
0.0491
\end{bmatrix}\;\text{N\,m}.
\end{equation}
The static hold torques at $q_0$ are therefore $[1847.610,\,495.075,\,49.050]^T$ N\,m.

\section{Task 2: Control-Law Partitioning}
Task 2 requires a closed-loop design split into model-based and servo components. The implemented control law is
\begin{equation}
\tau = M(q)\,v + C(q,\dot q)\dot q + G(q),
\quad
v = \ddot q_d + K_v(\dot q_d-\dot q) + K_p(q_d-q).
\label{eq:ctc}
\end{equation}
Substituting \eqref{eq:ctc} into \eqref{eq:eom} gives decoupled error dynamics,
\begin{equation}
\ddot e + K_v \dot e + K_p e = 0,
\quad e=q_d-q,
\end{equation}
which has second-order form at each joint.

Step tests are applied one joint at a time with $\Delta q=10^\circ$. Three proportional gains are used with critical damping choice $K_v=2\sqrt{K_p}$.

\section{Task 3: Joint-Space Trajectory Tracking}
The assignment trajectory is specified at $t=\{0,2,4,6,8\}$ s with waypoints for all three joints. Clamped cubic splines with zero initial and final slope are generated for each joint. Desired trajectories $(q_d,\dot q_d,\ddot q_d)$ are sampled online and injected into the partitioned controller in \eqref{eq:ctc}.

Two gain sets are compared to show transient differences while preserving endpoint tracking.
\begin{align*}
\text{High gain: } &K_p=100I,\quad K_v=20I,\\
\text{Lower gain: } &K_p=20I,\quad K_v=9I.
\end{align*}
A small initial velocity perturbation is used so that gain-dependent transient behavior is visible even under model-based feedforward.

Both runs complete the full \SI{8}{s} horizon and hit the required waypoints. The higher gain set drives faster decay of initial tracking error. The lower gain set shows longer transient tails.

\section{Task 5: Cartesian Controller}
Task 5 requests Cartesian command tracking in $x$, $y$, and $\alpha$. A Jacobian-based acceleration command is formed,
\begin{equation}
\ddot q_{cmd} = J^\dagger\left(\ddot x_d + D_x(\dot x_d-\dot x) + K_x(x_d-x) - \dot J\dot q\right),
\end{equation}
followed by inverse dynamics torque synthesis
\begin{equation}
\tau = M(q)\ddot q_{cmd} + C(q,\dot q)\dot q + G(q).
\end{equation}

A high-manipulability configuration is selected for demonstration,
\begin{equation*}
q=[80^\circ,-60^\circ,-40^\circ]^T,\qquad w=10.3923.
\end{equation*}
Independent commands in $x$, then $y$, then $\alpha$ are applied. Reported tracking RMS values from the run are
\begin{equation*}
\text{RMS}_x=\SI{0.0515}{m},\quad
\text{RMS}_y=\SI{0.0518}{m},\quad
\text{RMS}_{\alpha}=2.5345^\circ.
\end{equation*}
The Cartesian channels track the commanded sequence with stable recovery after each setpoint transition.

\section{Model Validation Notes}
The full-equilibrium Task 1 case verifies gravity consistency at the initial condition. Task 2 confirms the expected second-order form of the partitioned controller through rise-time and overshoot trends. Task 3 demonstrates spline tracking through all scheduled waypoints. Task 4 confirms physically consistent force-to-torque mapping through $J^TF$. Task 5 confirms that Cartesian-space commands can be executed with bounded joint torques in a favorable workspace region.

The open-loop energy drift observed in uncontrolled gravity cases is expected once viscous friction and strong nonlinear excursions are present. Those runs are not intended to preserve ideal mechanical energy. They are intended to show unforced manipulator behavior under gravity and partial compensation.
